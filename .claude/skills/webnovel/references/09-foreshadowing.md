# Foreshadowing Management Module

## Execution Flow

1. Plan the overall distribution and payoff pacing of three-tier foreshadowing based on the full-book outline
2. Register every planted foreshadowing in the foreshadowing ledger immediately
3. Update foreshadowing status in real-time as the plot progresses
4. Complete foreshadowing payoffs at planned nodes; mark payoff status
5. Review foreshadowing completion rate at each volume's end; adjust subsequent pacing
6. Sync to creative memory ledger

## Foreshadowing Three-Tier Classification

### Tier 1: Short-Term Foreshadowing

- **Payoff window**: Within 10 chapters
- **Purpose**: Chapter hooks, minor plot twists, detail callbacks
- **Density**: Plant 1 every 2–3 chapters

### Tier 2: Medium-Term Foreshadowing

- **Payoff window**: Within 1 volume
- **Purpose**: Volume climax twists, character identity reveals, arc-level plot reversals
- **Density**: 3–5 per volume

### Tier 3: Long-Term Foreshadowing

- **Payoff window**: Spanning the entire book / multiple volumes
- **Purpose**: World-truth reveal, ultimate twist, core main-plot suspense
- **Density**: 3–5 total for the entire book; planted at the opening, paid off at the ending

### II. Foreshadowing Planting Rules

1. **Natural integration**: Plant through character dialogue, detail actions, background description, passerby commentary — never overtly highlight
2. **Proportional subtlety**: Long-term foreshadowing should be lightly sketched; mid-term should leave traceable clues; short-term should be perceptible to readers
3. **No stacking**: Max 2 foreshadowings planted per chapter to avoid diluting reader attention
4. **Mandatory correspondence**: Every planted foreshadowing must correspond to a future payoff event; never dig meaningless holes

### III. Foreshadowing Payoff Rules

1. **Payoff must deliver**: Every foreshadowing payoff must bring a satisfaction beat, twist, or emotional impact — never a flat resolution
2. **Callback resonance**: When paying off, echo the planting details so readers feel "so that's what it was!"
3. **No overdue**: Strictly pay off at planned nodes; overdue foreshadowings must be annotated with reason and adjustment plan
4. **Tiered payoff**: Short-term pay off independently; mid-term pay off at volume climaxes; long-term pay off at the book's grand climax

## Foreshadowing Validation Checklist

- [ ] All foreshadowings are registered in the ledger; none missing
- [ ] Foreshadowing tiers are clear; payoff windows are defined; no unplanned digging
- [ ] Planting is natural; nothing feels forced or out of place
- [ ] Payoff has corresponding satisfaction beat / twist; value matches the setup investment
- [ ] No overdue foreshadowings without explanation
- [ ] Synced to creative memory ledger
